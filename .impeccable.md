## Design Context

### Users
Serious forex traders — both retail (daily/weekly traders tracking performance to find their edge) and prop firm traders (funded accounts needing discipline tracking and compliance tools). They arrive at the site looking for a professional-grade journal that goes beyond basic trade logging. They're skeptical of hype, value data, and make purchase decisions based on whether the tool actually solves their problem. Mac users who expect native-quality software.

### Brand Personality
**Professional, premium, trustworthy.** Dojix speaks like a sharp analyst — precise, confident, data-driven. No fluff, no hype, no motivational trading quotes. The interface should feel like a tool built by someone who actually trades, not a marketing team. Earns trust through quality and depth, not promises.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Warm dark terminal aesthetic — not cold/clinical dark mode, but warm brown-blacks with gold accent. Think: private trading desk, not crypto Discord.
- **Theme**: Dark only. Warm gold (#e8b44c) on brown-black (#0e0d0b). Warm-tinted borders and surfaces.
- **Fonts**: DM Sans (body/headings) + JetBrains Mono (data/stats/prices).
- **References**: Bloomberg terminal's information density + Stripe's polish + Linear's clean dark UI.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS templates (hero + 3 cards + pricing), crypto/Web3 neon glow sites, cheap trading tool ads (red/green candles, "make $10K/day" energy), overdesigned fintech (glassmorphism, excessive animation, style over substance).

### Design Principles
1. **Show, don't tell** — Screenshots and real data speak louder than marketing copy. Let the product sell itself.
2. **Density signals depth** — Traders respect tools with substance. Don't oversimplify to the point of looking shallow.
3. **Warm, not cold** — Dark theme should feel inviting and premium, not sterile or intimidating. The warm gold palette exists for this reason.
4. **Earn trust through specifics** — Dollar amounts, exact feature counts, concrete comparisons. Vague claims feel like every other trading tool.
5. **Respect the visitor's intelligence** — These are people who understand MAE/MFE, R-multiples, and kill zones. Don't talk down to them, but don't gatekeep either.
